## Core Description

A Medical Science Liaison (MSL) serves as a **field-based scientific expert** who establishes and maintains **peer-to-peer relationships** with Key Opinion Leaders (KOLs), healthcare professionals, and academic institutions.

Unlike sales representatives, MSLs are **non-promotional professionals** who engage in scientific exchange, providing balanced, evidence-based information on therapeutic areas and clinical data. MSLs gather real-world insights from the field that inform internal teams including medical affairs, clinical development, and regulatory strategy.

## Typical Scenarios

### Scenario 1: KOL Scientific Exchange
Dr. Chen schedules a meeting with a leading oncologist at an academic medical center to discuss recent Phase 3 trial data. She prepares by reviewing the latest publications and anticipating scientific questions. During the meeting, she provides balanced data interpretation while gathering insights about clinical practice patterns in the region.

### Scenario 2: Congress Attendance
At ASCO, Dr. Chen attends scientific sessions, visits the company booth (in a non-promotional capacity), and schedules one-on-one meetings with KOLs. She documents insights in the medical insights platform and reports an adverse event mentioned by a physician within 24 hours.

### Scenario 3: Investigator-Initiated Trial Support
A researcher at a teaching hospital wants to conduct an IIT. Dr. Chen provides scientific consultation on study design, connects the researcher with clinical development, and helps navigate the internal process while maintaining appropriate documentation.
