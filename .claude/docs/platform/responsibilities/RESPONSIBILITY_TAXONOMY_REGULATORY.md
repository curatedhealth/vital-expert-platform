## Overview

- **15 cross-cutting responsibilities** shared across roles and levels

## Role-Specific Responsibilities (35)

### Leadership & Strategy (4)

**RESP-RA-001: Global Regulatory Strategy Ownership**
- **Name**: Global Regulatory Strategy Ownership
- **Definition**: Own and execute comprehensive global regulatory strategy across all products, therapeutic areas, and markets with full accountability for approvals and compliance.
- **Level Scope**: L1 MASTER
- **Accountability**: Strategic plan execution, global approval targets (>90%), regulatory compliance (100%), stakeholder alignment

**RESP-RA-002: Regional Regulatory Leadership & P&L**
- **Name**: Regional Regulatory Leadership & P&L
- **Definition**: Lead regional regulatory function with P&L accountability, team management, and responsibility for achieving submission and approval milestones.
- **Level Scope**: L1 MASTER, L2 EXPERT
- **Accountability**: Regional approval targets, budget adherence (±5%), team performance, compliance rate (100%)

**RESP-RA-003: Therapeutic Area Regulatory Leadership**
- **Name**: Therapeutic Area Regulatory Leadership
- **Definition**: Direct all regulatory activities for assigned therapeutic area including strategy, submissions, agency interactions, and team leadership.
- **Level Scope**: L2 EXPERT
- **Accountability**: TA approval milestones, submission quality, agency relationships, team development

**RESP-RA-004: Regulatory Project Delivery**
- **Name**: Regulatory Project Delivery
- **Definition**: Deliver regulatory projects on time, on budget, with high quality including submissions, approvals, and lifecycle management.
- **Level Scope**: L2 EXPERT, L3 SPECIALIST
- **Accountability**: Project timeline adherence (>95%), submission quality (zero critical deficiencies), budget management
