## Market Intelligence Highlights

### Competition Landscape
- **Veeva** launching AI agents December 2025, but lacks multi-agent orchestration
- **Anthropic** released Claude for Life Sciences October 2025, but it's a general LLM not a platform
- **Microsoft** offers generic healthcare orchestrator requiring heavy customization
- **Medidata** focuses on clinical trials, minimal medical affairs presence

### Key Market Insights
1. **Pricing shifting** from per-seat to outcome-based models (73% of AI companies experimenting)
2. **HITL (Human-in-the-Loop)** now required for production deployments in regulated industries
3. **Medical affairs** emerging as top AI investment priority (40% of pharma budgets)
4. **ROI expectations** aggressive - 14-month payback required by enterprises
5. **Integration complexity** is primary barrier to adoption

## Strategic Pivots Required

### 1. Focus: Horizontal → Vertical
**From**: Building for all healthcare segments
**To**: Deep focus on pharma medical affairs first
**Why**: Highest ROI, clearest pain points, 40% of AI investment

### 2. Go-to-Market: Direct → Partnership
**From**: Direct sales to enterprises
**To**: Integrate with Veeva, Medidata, Salesforce platforms
**Why**: Faster adoption, lower CAC, leverage existing relationships

### 3. Pricing: Subscription → Hybrid Outcome-Based
**From**: $5K-50K/month flat subscription
**To**: $2K base + usage + success bonuses
**Why**: Market demand, higher margins (75% vs 60%), better alignment
