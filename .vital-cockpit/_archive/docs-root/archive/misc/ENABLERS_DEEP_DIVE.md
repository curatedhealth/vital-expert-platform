### **1. CAPITAL PROVIDERS (3 Personas, ~15 JTBDs, $100M Value)**

These are the **investors and financial backers** who fund digital health innovation.

#### **Personas:**

**a) Digital Health VC Partner**
- **Who**: Venture capital firm partner specializing in digital health
- **Role**: Investment decision-making, portfolio management
- **Key JTBDs**:
  - Evaluate digital health investment opportunities
  - Assess technical feasibility and clinical validity
  - Support portfolio companies with strategic guidance
  - Connect startups with pharma/payer partners
  - Drive exits (M&A, IPO)

**b) Corporate Venture Director**
- **Who**: Pharma/health system corporate venture arm leader
- **Role**: Strategic investments aligned with corporate goals
- **Key JTBDs**:
  - Identify strategic digital health investments
  - Structure partnership + investment deals
  - Integrate acquired capabilities
  - Manage innovation pipeline
  - Report ROI to corporate leadership

**c) Angel Investor / Advisory Board Member**
- **Who**: Individual investors, often former industry executives
- **Role**: Early-stage funding, mentorship, connections
- **Key JTBDs**:
  - Source promising early-stage opportunities
  - Provide strategic advice to founders
  - Open doors to key decision-makers
  - Validate market opportunities
  - Support fundraising rounds

- **Role**: Hands-on implementation, workflow design, integration
- **Key JTBDs**:

  - Integrate digital solutions with existing systems

  - Measure and optimize implementation success

- **Who**: Organizational change specialists for digital adoption
- **Role**: Culture change, stakeholder engagement, adoption
- **Key JTBDs**:

  - Train clinicians and staff on digital tools
  - Measure and improve user adoption rates

### **3. TECHNOLOGY PARTNERS (3 Personas, ~12 JTBDs, $50M Value)**

These are the **infrastructure and platform providers** that enable digital health solutions to function.

#### **Personas:**

**a) EHR Integration Partner**
- **Who**: Epic, Cerner, Allscripts integration teams/vendors
- **Role**: Enable digital health solutions to connect with EHRs
- **Key JTBDs**:
  - Design and build EHR integration APIs
  - Ensure FHIR/HL7 standards compliance
  - Support digital health vendors with integration
  - Troubleshoot data exchange issues
  - Maintain integration security and performance

**b) Cloud Platform Provider (Healthcare)**
- **Who**: AWS Healthcare, Azure Health, Google Cloud Healthcare
- **Role**: Provide compliant infrastructure for digital health
- **Key JTBDs**:
  - Deliver HIPAA-compliant cloud infrastructure
  - Enable scalable, secure data storage
  - Support AI/ML model deployment
  - Ensure high availability and disaster recovery
  - Optimize costs for digital health workloads

**c) API / Interoperability Vendor**
- **Who**: Redox, Health Gorilla, Particle Health, etc.
- **Role**: Simplify healthcare data exchange
- **Key JTBDs**:
  - Provide unified API for healthcare data access
  - Normalize data across different sources
  - Enable bi-directional data flow
  - Support multiple data standards (FHIR, HL7, X12)
  - Ensure data quality and completeness

**Why They Matter**:
- ğŸ”— **Connectivity**: Enable integration and interoperability
- ğŸ—ï¸ **Infrastructure**: Provide foundation for digital solutions
- ğŸ”’ **Compliance**: Ensure HIPAA, security, and regulatory adherence
- âš¡ **Performance**: Enable scale and reliability
- ğŸ’° **Economics**: Reduce infrastructure costs and complexity

### **4. RESEARCH & ACADEMIA (2 Personas, ~8 JTBDs, $30M Value)**

These are the **academic researchers and institutions** that validate digital health solutions and advance the science.

#### **Personas:**

**a) Clinical Research Principal Investigator (PI)**
- **Who**: Academic physician-scientist leading clinical trials
- **Role**: Conduct clinical research on digital health interventions
- **Key JTBDs**:
  - Design clinical trials for digital therapeutics
  - Secure research funding (NIH, foundations)
  - Publish clinical evidence in peer-reviewed journals
  - Present findings at academic conferences
  - Advise digital health companies on research design

**b) Health Services Researcher**
- **Who**: PhD researcher studying health systems and outcomes
- **Role**: Study effectiveness, cost-effectiveness, and implementation
- **Key JTBDs**:
  - Evaluate real-world effectiveness of digital health
  - Conduct health economics and outcomes research (HEOR)
  - Study implementation science and adoption barriers
  - Assess health equity impacts of digital solutions
  - Inform policy and reimbursement decisions
