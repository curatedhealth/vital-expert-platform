## Level 1: VP Medical Affairs

### Strategic Leadership Responsibilities (12)

1. **Build Quality Medical Affairs Organization** - Focus on maximizing patient benefit from branded therapies through safe and appropriate use
2. **Set Strategic Direction** - Collaborate with Commercial, Clinical Research, and Product Management for all franchises and branded therapies
3. **Cross-Functional Partnership** - Partner with R&D, Commercial, Market Access, and Corporate Affairs to embed medical affairs priorities
4. **Lead Global Medical Initiatives** - Support product launches, lifecycle management, and external engagement
5. **Oversee Medical Affairs Teams** - Manage direct reports including medical communications, publications, medical information, HEOR, and field medical
6. **Build Team for Growing Portfolio** - Recruit and manage MA team to support expanding product portfolio
7. **Financial Management** - Accountability for financial management and reporting requirements for Medical Affairs function
8. **Budget Optimization** - Manage medical affairs budget and ensure optimal resource allocation
9. **Publication Strategy Leadership** - Set up publication strategy and oversee management of publication planning and execution
10. **Ensure Fair and Balanced Communication** - Ensure all data generated by company or investigator-sponsored studies is publicly presented appropriately and in timely fashion
11. **Drive Medical Innovation Culture** - Ensure emerging scientific trends and digital health technologies are incorporated into medical affairs strategies
12. **Champion Novel Approaches** - Promote digital platforms and data-driven insights to optimize impact

### External Engagement & Compliance (3)

13. **Build KOL Relationships** - Instrumental in building relationships with key opinion leaders, healthcare providers, and academic institutions
14. **Strengthen Scientific Credibility** - Oversee external engagement strategies to enhance interactions with HCPs, regulators, and patient advocacy groups
15. **Ensure Regulatory Compliance** - Ensure all medical affairs activities adhere to regulatory and ethical standards; serve as key liaison with stakeholders

### Required Qualifications

- MD (or MD, PhD), or PharmD with substantial experience; Board certification not required
- 15+ years experience in pharmaceutical/biotech industry
- Minimum of 10 to 15 years of experience in medical affairs
- Solid working knowledge of regulatory and compliance environment

## Level 2: Department Heads

### 2A. Head of MSL Operations

#### Primary Responsibilities (15)

1. **Strategic MSL Program Leadership** - Develop and execute MSL strategy aligned with medical affairs goals
2. **KOL Engagement Strategy** - Design and implement KOL identification, tiering, and engagement frameworks
3. **Territory Management** - Oversee geographic territory assignments and coverage optimization
4. **Field Team Performance** - Manage MSL team performance, training, and professional development
5. **Scientific Exchange Oversight** - Ensure peer-to-peer scientific exchange maintains non-promotional integrity
6. **Clinical Trial Support** - Coordinate MSL involvement in investigator-initiated trials and site selection
7. **Congress Planning** - Develop congress coverage strategy and coordinate MSL activities at scientific meetings
8. **Advisory Board Coordination** - Oversee advisory board logistics, execution, and insights capture
9. **Insights Management** - Establish systems for capturing, analyzing, and disseminating field insights
10. **Cross-Functional Collaboration** - Partner with medical affairs, clinical development, regulatory, and commercial teams
11. **Medical Information Support** - Ensure MSLs provide accurate, evidence-based responses to medical inquiries
12. **Budget Management** - Manage MSL program budget including travel, congress, and advisory board expenses
13. **Compliance Oversight** - Ensure all MSL activities comply with regulatory requirements and company policies
14. **Digital Enablement** - Implement digital tools and platforms to enhance MSL efficiency
15. **Stakeholder Alignment** - Facilitate communication between MSLs and internal stakeholders

#### Required Competencies

- Deep therapeutic area expertise (oncology, immunology, gene therapy, rare diseases)
- Strong relationship-building and interpersonal skills
- Understanding of clinical trial design and execution
- Knowledge of regulatory environment (FDA, EMA, ICH)
- Typically PharmD or PhD with 8+ years pharmaceutical experience

### 2D. Head of Pharmacovigilance/Safety

#### Primary Responsibilities (15)

1. **Pharmacovigilance Program Leadership** - Develop and execute comprehensive pharmacovigilance strategy
2. **ICH Guideline Implementation** - Ensure compliance with ICH E2A-E2F guidelines (expedited reporting, data management, PSURs)
3. **FDA Compliance Management** - Analyze and implement FDA, EU, and ICH regulations governing pharmacovigilance
4. **Adverse Event Coding** - Ensure accurate, prompt, consistent coding of AE data using MedDRA and WHO Drug dictionaries
5. **SUSAR Assessment** - Responsible for medical assessment of Suspected Unexpected Serious Adverse Reactions
6. **Statistical Compliance** - Ensure statistical procedures align with GCP, GEP, GVP and regulatory requirements
7. **Safety Reporting** - Manage expedited reporting of adverse drug reactions and individual case safety reports
8. **Periodic Safety Updates** - Oversee creation of Periodic Safety Update Reports (PSURs) and Periodic Benefit-Risk Evaluation Reports
9. **Post-Market Surveillance** - Establish systems for post-market safety data collection and analysis
10. **Signal Detection** - Implement signal detection methodologies and adverse event monitoring systems
11. **Risk Management Plans** - Develop and maintain Risk Evaluation and Mitigation Strategies (REMS)
12. **Cross-Reporting Management** - Ensure cross-reporting to IIT/ISR investigators
13. **Safety Database Management** - Oversee global safety database operations and data integrity
14. **Regulatory Authority Liaison** - Serve as primary contact with FDA, EMA, and other regulatory bodies on safety matters
15. **Safety Training** - Train internal teams on pharmacovigilance requirements and reporting obligations

#### Required Qualifications

- Medical degree (MD, PharmD) or advanced degree in life sciences
- Deep knowledge of ICH guidelines (E2A-E2F) and GVP
- Understanding of MedDRA and WHO Drug coding systems
- Experience with global safety databases
- Knowledge of statistical methods for safety analysis

### 2F. Head of KOL Management

#### Primary Responsibilities (13)

1. **KOL Identification Strategy** - Develop systematic approach to identify KOLs with influence and expertise in therapeutic areas
2. **KOL Tiering Framework** - Implement tiering strategy (Tier 1/2/3) to prioritize engagement and resource allocation
3. **Engagement Planning** - Design comprehensive KOL engagement plans with clear objectives and tactics
4. **Relationship Building** - Build strong connections through advisory boards, scientific summits, consultancies, publications
5. **KOL Mapping Excellence** - Ensure accurate KOL rosters (addressing common 30%+ error rates in KOL mapping)
6. **Digital Engagement** - Leverage social media (LinkedIn, Twitter, Sermo, Doximity) for KOL identification and engagement
7. **Multi-Touchpoint Strategy** - Coordinate advisory boards, scientific summits, internal training, publications, media activities, speaking engagements
8. **Insights Capture** - Gather and synthesize KOL insights to inform medical strategy
9. **Cross-Functional Integration** - Ensure KOL insights embedded into medical affairs, clinical development, and commercial strategy
10. **Ethical Standards Maintenance** - Maintain transparency and ethical conduct in all KOL collaborations
11. **Resource Provision** - Offer KOLs necessary resources for research, conferences, and staying current in field
12. **Two-Way Communication** - Foster open communication, actively seek input and feedback on strategy
13. **Broader Stakeholder Expansion** - Evolve beyond traditional KOLs to engage wider array of stakeholders for better insights

#### Best Practices

- Set clear objectives for each KOL engagement
- Focus Tier 1 resources on highest-impact KOLs
- Use data-driven approaches for KOL identification
- Maintain compliance with industry regulations
- Track engagement metrics and ROI

### 4B. Context Engineer (AI Augmentation Specialist)

#### Primary Responsibilities (12)

1. **AI System Context Building** - Build systems to help AI use the right information for medical affairs tasks
2. **Knowledge Base Architecture** - Organize what AI remembers so it can use past actions for better decisions
3. **Context Update Systems** - Build systems that update AI knowledge as new medical/scientific facts emerge
4. **Retention Rules Development** - Make rules for what AI should keep, forget, or find during medical affairs tasks
5. **Integration Planning** - Work with teams to ensure AI tools fit into real medical affairs workflows
6. **Prompt Engineering** - Design and optimize prompts for medical affairs use cases
7. **Testing and Improvement** - Test and improve how AI uses context for better answers
8. **AI Augmentation Strategy** - Identify opportunities to augment (not replace) medical affairs professionals
9. **Insights Automation** - Automate aggregation and analysis of data from publications, congresses, HCP interactions
10. **Compliance Oversight** - Ensure AI-generated outputs comply with regulatory and ethical standards
11. **Human Review Protocols** - Develop protocols for human oversight of AI recommendations
12. **Training and Enablement** - Train medical affairs teams on AI tools, prompting techniques, and limitations

#### Key Principles

- AI augments MSL capabilities, does not replace them
- Human oversight remains essential for accuracy, safety, and ethics
- Each professional should be empowered to experiment with AI augmentation of their tasks
- Basic training on prompting (context, tone, audience, length) is essential
- Understanding AI limitations and biases is critical

- Expanded focus on post-market surveillance

- Stricter post-market reporting obligations to monitor adverse events
- Enhanced digital health monitoring tools for post-market data collection

#### Quality Systems
- Focus on systemic quality issues rather than isolated procedural failures
- Emphasis on organizational culture and comprehensive compliance programs
- Intensified scrutiny of foreign manufacturers

#### Lifecycle Management Approach
- Shift toward lifecycle management vs. point-of-approval focus
- Earlier engagement with sponsors during product development
- Harmonization of clinical endpoints and patient cohorts
- Enhanced requirements for confirmatory trials post-accelerated approval

- PreCheck Initiative to streamline U.S. facility establishment

### ICH Guidelines for Pharmacovigilance

#### Key ICH E-Series Guidelines

**E2A - Clinical Safety Data Management**
- Definitions and standards for expedited reporting
- Adverse drug reaction reporting requirements

**E2B - Data Elements for Transmission**
- Maintenance of clinical safety data management
- Individual case safety report (ICSR) data elements

**E2C - Periodic Safety Update Reports**
- PSUR requirements for marketed drugs
- Periodic Benefit-Risk Evaluation Report (PBRER)

**E2D - Post-Approval Safety Data Management**
- Post-marketing surveillance requirements
- Ongoing safety monitoring

**E2E - Pharmacovigilance Planning**
- Safety Specification requirements
- Pharmacovigilance Plan development
- Preparation for early postmarketing period

**E2F - Development Safety Update Report**
- DSUR requirements during development
- Benefit-risk assessment during development

#### Definition
WHO definition: "The science and activities relating to the detection, assessment, understanding and prevention of adverse effects or any other drug related problems."

## Summary of Key Findings

### Role Hierarchy Clarity
1. **L1 (VP)** - Strategic leadership, cross-functional partnership, financial management, global initiatives
2. **L2 (Heads)** - Functional leadership, team management, tactical execution, compliance oversight
3. **L3 (Specialists)** - Domain expertise, hands-on execution, scientific excellence, field insights
4. **L4 (Workers/Context Engineers)** - Operational support, coordination, AI augmentation, process optimization
5. **L5 (Tools)** - Atomic operations, automation, templates, databases

### Critical Success Factors
- **Evidence-based decision making** at all levels
- **Regulatory compliance** as non-negotiable foundation
- **Cross-functional collaboration** across medical affairs ecosystem
- **Digital enablement** and AI augmentation without replacing human expertise
- **Continuous learning** through MAPS, DIA, and other professional development

### 2025 Trends
- **Increased FDA scrutiny** on post-market surveillance and quality systems
- **AI augmentation** becoming standard practice in medical affairs
- **Real-world evidence** generation as strategic priority
- **Patient-centricity** gaining prominence
- **Digital engagement** expanding beyond traditional channels
- **Lifecycle management** approach replacing point-of-approval focus
